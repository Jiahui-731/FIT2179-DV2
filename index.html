Can We Swim Today?

Every summer, millions of Melburnians flock to the beaches of Port Phillip Bay. But the water isn't always as clean as it looks. Using six years of EPA Victoria sampling and Bureau of Meteorology rainfall data, this is a story about when, where, and why it's actually safe to swim.

By Jiahui · 5,416 routine samples · 36 beaches · 2020-2025

Three things the data already knows

  1. Across 5,416 routine samples, 10.2% fail EPA's swim-safe threshold — but the failures cluster, badly.
  2. The single biggest predictor isn't the season but yesterday's rain: more than 20mm nearly doubles the share of failed samples.
  3. Eastern Bayside beaches got dramatically dirtier between 2020-2022 and 2023-2025; several Inner-North beaches actually improved.

③ When

The dirtiest months are the ones we swim in.

The dirtiest beaches peak in late summer and early autumn. That's exactly when people swim.

Ten beaches moved by a meaningful margin. Five for the worse, five for the better.

Some months are bay-wide disasters. Four months saw over a quarter of samples fail.

④ Why

The biggest predictor is whether it rained yesterday. Each dot is one sample; the dashed line, the trend.

⑤ Region

Geography beats timing.

Western Shore has half the bacteria of the Inner North. The Mornington Peninsula isn't the clean haven you'd expect.
